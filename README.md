**Cancer immunized vascularized assembloids**

The tumor microenvironment emerges from dynamic crosstalk among vasculature, immune, and stromal compartments, yet existing human models rarely capture these interactions. We developed cancer-immunized vascularized assembloids (CIVAs) by differentiating human pluripotent stem cells into vascular–immune organoids and fusing them with patient-derived tumor organoids. CIVAs were robustly generated across lung, colorectal, glioblastoma, and hepatocellular carcinoma samples, yielding immunosuppressive IL1B+ microenvironment. Spatially organized oncofetal ecosystem featured by FOLR2+ tumor-associated macrophages, POSTN+ cancer associated fibroblasts, and PLVAP+ endothelial cells was formed. CIVA recapitulated resistance to TKI, that targeting KDR reversed the resistance via disrupting oncofetal ecosystem. Together, these data establish CIVAs as a patient-precision platform that discovered the oncofetal ecosystem as targetable.
