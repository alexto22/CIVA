**Cancer immunized vascularized assembloids**

The tumor microenvironment emerges from dynamic crosstalk among vasculature, immune, and stromal compartments, yet existing human models rarely capture these interactions. We developed cancer-immunized vascularized assembloids (CIVAs) by differentiating human pluripotent stem cells into vascular–immune organoids and fusing them with patient-derived tumor organoids. CIVAs were robustly generated across lung, colorectal, glioblastoma, and hepatocellular carcinoma samples, yielding reproducible constructs with functional vascularization. Multimodal profiling revealed spatially organized immune-high and immune-low stromal niches that recapitulate features of patient tumors. CIVAs captured pathologic myeloid and stromal states, including IL1B+ macrophages and oncofetal fibroblast niches, and enabled mechanistic dissection of niche assembly. Osimertinib and Ramucirumab dual blocking shows effective tumor killing that reduce oncofetal niche formation within the assembloids. Together, these data establish CIVAs as a scalable, patient-precision platform that models tumor vasculature–immune–stroma interactions, supports discovery of niche drivers, and provides a tractable system to evaluate strategies that reprogram the tumor microenvironment.
